A transient electrical stimulation changes a retained tissue state q(t).
After the stimulus is removed, later wound repair and morphology remain
predictable from q. The boundary B is the membrane, wound edge, and tissue
niche. A closure residual C(q,u,B)=0 tests whether the state is admissible.

partial_t q = W(u,B,t)-q/tau_q
C(q,u,B)=0
y(t)=A(q,B)

The mechanism fails if washout, state erasure, or boundary exchange leaves the
later phenotype unchanged.
